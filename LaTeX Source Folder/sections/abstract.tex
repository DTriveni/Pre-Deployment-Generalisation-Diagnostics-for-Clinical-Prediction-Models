\begin{abstract}
Machine learning models used in healthcare often stop working when they are applied to patients or settings they were not trained on. This portfolio reviews over fifteen papers, critically analyses six key studies, and identifies the main unsolved problem in the field: researchers can explain why models fail after deployment, but they cannot predict whether a model will fail before it is used on new patients. To address this, I propose a three-stage framework that measures how different a new patient population is from the training data, checks which features in the model are reliable across different settings, and provides clear guidance on whether the model should be deployed, adjusted, or withheld.
\end{abstract}
